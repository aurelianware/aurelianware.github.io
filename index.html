The Problem

Legacy vendor lock-in is choking
healthcare innovation

A single vendor bought control of the et and independent implementation partners were displaced overnight. Health plans found themselves captive to a this vendor's roadmap, pricing, and compliance timeline.

CMS-0057-F Mandate

The CMS Interoperability and Prior Authorization final rule requires health plans to implement FHIR-based prior auth APIs. Existing CAPS vendors are slow to deliver compliant modules — and charge a premium when they do.

Vendor-Locked Compliance

Adding interoperability shouldn't mean replacing your entire claims adjudication system. Yet that's exactly what most vendors demand — a multi-year, multi-million dollar rip-and-replace.

Displaced Partners

Hundreds of experienced TriZetto implementation consultants lost access to the platform they built their careers on. Their expertise has no home in the Cognizant-controlled ecosystem.

Innovation Stagnation

Closed-source payer systems move at the vendor's pace, not yours. Feature requests take years. Bug fixes take months. And you pay for every change order.
